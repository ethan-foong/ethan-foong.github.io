<p>Just two ideas from me today:</p><ol><li>Changing your mind is a systemic process. When you're young or don't know what you're talking about, it's easy to latch on to the first reasonable-sounding argument. But, as you hear new evidence, each argument is merely a new data point to add to the collection of world views you've encountered.<ol><li>The challenge here is objectively gauging the value of each argument and preventing yourself from ever being exposed to arguments that oppose your worldview.</li><li><strong>In short, one argument shouldn't change your mind, but if a large group of experts can back themselves up with data, maybe it's time to hear them out.</strong></li></ol></li></ol><p></p><ol start="2"><li>One day of mental clarity is priceless. <ol><li>If you can, schedule one day with nothing to do once a quarter, or once a month to ask yourself how your current endeavors align with your goals and values.</li><li>In addition, think about yourself and how you fit into the community of people around you. Who's beneficial to your state of mind? Who's life are you making better?</li></ol></li></ol><p>See you next week,</p><p>-Ethan</p>
